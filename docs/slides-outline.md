# Four-slide pitch outline

## Slide 1 — The opacity problem

- Headline: **A precise-looking ancestry result can hide an unequal evidence base.**
- Consumer reference panels and genomics studies are unevenly representative.
- Consequence: less transferable evidence and responsibly less-specific
  explanations for underrepresented groups.
- Positioning: **not ancestry inference—an audit layer for an existing result.**

Visual: one restrained comparison from the app’s cited reference-panel chart.

## Slide 2 — The product moment

- Upload an exported 23andMe text file.
- Supply the broad label already shown by the consumer test.
- Receive a deterministic report:
  - format and chip-coverage transparency;
  - allowlisted non-medical trait explanations;
  - evidence and population-transfer caveats;
  - default-deny boundaries;
  - dated research-program bridge.

Visual: one screenshot showing a trait card beside the honest-limits card.

## Slide 3 — Safety by architecture

- No ancestry inference from raw genotypes.
- No health, disease-risk, or exact-ethnicity conclusions.
- Only explicitly reviewed non-medical markers are interpreted.
- User-supplied broad context is kept separate from genetic data.
- Uploads are handled in memory and are not retained by application code.
- Study links are dated and direct users to official consent/privacy information.

Visual: `upload → validate → allowlist → limitations → report`, with every other
variant taking the default-deny branch.

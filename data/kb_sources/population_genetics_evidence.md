# Population-genetics evidence dossier

Curated 2026-07-11 for the Ancestry Audit Layer research agent. This document
is an index into open peer-reviewed sources, not a replacement for them. Use the
linked article when citing a claim. Population labels describe study sampling
and are not biological borders, ethnic identities, or ancestry percentages.

## Interpreting a broad result

A reference-panel distance is a similarity measure over overlapping markers. It
is not an ancestry percentage or identity verdict. The 1000 Genomes Phase 3
reference contains 2,504 people across 26 named sampling populations and five
superpopulations; that scope limits the specificity of any comparison.

- 1000 Genomes Project Consortium, *A global reference for human genetic
  variation*, Nature, 2015. Purpose: baseline panel composition, variation, and
  sampling scope. Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4750478/

## Why panel representation changes confidence

Genotype imputation and reference comparison depend on sample count, haplotype
diversity, marker overlap, and how well the study population is represented.
For 412 Thai participants, GenomeAsia produced the highest reported median
genotype concordance among four compared panels despite being smaller, showing
that ancestry matching can matter alongside raw panel size.

- Mauleekoonphairoj et al., *A diverse ancestrally-matched reference panel
  increases genotype imputation accuracy in an underrepresented population*,
  Scientific Reports, 2023. Purpose: measured panel-performance comparison in
  a Thai cohort. Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10390539/
- Corpas et al., *Bridging genomics' greatest challenge: The diversity gap*,
  Cell Genomics, 2025. Purpose: representation, trust, and research-ethics
  context. Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11770215/

## Population history and overlap

Human variation is continuous and migration and admixture are widespread.
Broad geographic labels compress that history. GenomeAsia's pilot sampled
1,739 people from 219 population groups across 64 countries, illustrating
substantial diversity within labels commonly reduced to “Asian.” Qatari whole
genomes likewise show regional structure, migration, and admixture that a
single “Middle Eastern” bucket cannot represent.

- GenomeAsia100K Consortium, *The GenomeAsia 100K Project enables genetic
  discoveries across Asia*, Nature, 2019. Purpose: South and East Asian
  diversity and sampling breadth. Source:
  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7054211/
- Thareja et al., *Thousands of Qatari genomes inform human migration history
  and improve imputation of Arab haplotypes*, Nature Communications, 2021.
  Purpose: 6,218 whole genomes and a 12,432-haplotype Arab/Middle Eastern
  reference panel. Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8511259/

## Underrepresented and admixed populations

Panel names should not be treated as homogeneous. A panel dominated by one
region can underperform elsewhere within the same continent. Admixed genomes
also vary along the genome, so a global summary can hide local ancestry and
reference mismatch.

- Sengupta et al., *Evaluating imputation reference panels for South African
  populations*, Human Genetics and Genomics Advances, 2023. Purpose: measured
  limitations when nominally African resources overrepresent West African
  variation. Source:
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666979X23001003
- O'Connell et al., *A population-specific reference panel for improved
  genotype imputation in African Americans*, Nature Communications, 2021.
  Purpose: 2,269-person panel with Atlantic African, European, and American
  admixture. Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8571350/
- Jiménez-Kaufmann et al., *Imputation performance in Latin American
  populations*, Communications Biology, 2022. Purpose: measured effect of 134
  Native American genomes and explicit Mexico-heavy limitations. Source:
  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8762266/
- Borda et al., *Genetics of Latin American Diversity Project*, Cell Genomics,
  2024. Purpose: 53,738 participants across 39 studies and 46 regions, admixed
  population structure, consent/access constraints, and privacy-preserving
  summary-statistic matching. Source:
  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11605695/
- Gao et al., *A reference panel of 14,393 East Asian genomes*, Nature Genetics,
  2023. Purpose: low-frequency variant discovery and improved East Asian
  imputation. Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10411914/

## Trait-evidence and safety boundary

The app's allowed traits are non-medical illustrations. A measured genotype can
support only the narrow statement defined for that marker; it does not establish
a person's identity, culture, health, or deterministic phenotype. Missing or
unvalidated markers must remain missing. Health-risk interpretation, APOE/BRCA,
exact ethnicity, and unsupported extrapolation are outside the agent contract.

## Research participation and consent

Participation and data-access pathways differ by program. The research bridge
must distinguish public enrollment from a consortium or controlled-data
resource, show dates, and link to the official program. Historical and current
underrepresentation does not justify treating any person as a data source;
informed consent, governance, community partnership, and the right not to
participate remain essential.
